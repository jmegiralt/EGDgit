Beyond Digital: De-Code

Created by John Smith / 18th May 2013

ECO Design Digital Future Electrolux Ideas

This is the beginning of a new era where digital will not be separated from physical anymore, but both will merge into a dynamic reality of mixed experiences. We will discover new ways of organizing the vast amounts of information accumulated by our virtual lifestyles. Many forgotten attributes of our offline lives will return to the daily experience, giving breath to an updated tangible and visual culture.
In our contemporary society, the meaning of objects and experiences is placed in-between the virtual and the physical realms. A new generation of design hybrids is emerging, aiming to bridge the gap between the two worlds by combining robot-readable language with tangible objects.

!The Mnemosyne! by Michael Eden incorporates a QR code that connects a box to a web page of digitally stored memories and treasures.
!Clocks for Robots! by BERG London is a product sketch that signals both time and place to artificial eyes.
!Instances! by Tal Erez and Oded Lahav visually bridges physical objects with digital experiences. Each part of a vase can be a separate object with its own QR code.
The !Cabinet of the (material and virtual) world ! is a design project by Jon Stam combining physical objects and digital presence. It brings together both the material and virtual worlds with sixteen drawers for physical archiving on one face and sixteen corresponding engraved patterns linking to digital data such as photos or videos on the other.
